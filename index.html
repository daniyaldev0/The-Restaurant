Our Story

Every Dish Begins With a Story.

Long before Volterra opened its doors, it existed in the quiet rituals of a Tuscan kitchen — the scrape of a wooden spoon against copper, rosemary crushed between fingers, bread proofing beneath a linen cloth. Those rituals travel with us still, carried into every service.

Each morning begins before the dining room opens — with a market visit, a call to a fisherman working the coast near Positano, or the arrival of olive oil pressed only weeks earlier in Umbria. Nothing reaches the table without first being tasted, questioned, and tasted again.

The result isn't a menu. It's an inheritance, plated with restraint and served without hurry.

Our Executive Chef

Meet the Vision Behind Every Plate

A philosophy built on restraint as much as skill — where precision, instinct, and the honesty of the season shape every plate that leaves the kitchen.

Alexander Moreau Executive Chef

Trained in the stone-walled kitchens of Florence before crossing into the exacting discipline of two Michelin-starred houses in Lyon, Alexander has spent over two decades refining a singular philosophy: that restraint, not spectacle, is the truest form of mastery. At Volterra, he leads with instinct as much as technique — tasting a sauce a dozen times before it reaches a plate, walking the market before dawn to let the season dictate the menu rather than the calendar. His dishes carry an unmistakable clarity: each ingredient permitted to speak once, and clearly. Under his direction, the kitchen operates less like a factory and more like an atelier, where every plate departs only when it has earned the right to.
